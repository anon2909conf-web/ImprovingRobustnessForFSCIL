Abstract

Few-shot class-incremental learning (FSCIL) aims to enable models to continually learn new classes from limited data while retaining performance on previously learned ones. Existing approaches typically freeze the feature extractor during incremental updates, but do not explicitly ensure that base-class representations remain sufficiently separable. As a result, features of newly introduced classes may overlap with those of earlier classes.


We propose Dataset-Aware Adaptive Augmentation (DA²), a feature-level augmentation strategy that strengthens and diversifies base-class feature distributions prior to incremental learning. Our method generates multi-domain augmented features through (i) spatial mixing, (ii) frequency-domain perturbations, and (iii) controlled noise. Crucially, we compute the mean inter-class cosine similarity of base-class prototypes in a single forward pass and adapt the augmentation regime accordingly — applying the full multi-domain pipeline for coarse-grained datasets and a spatial-only regime for fine-grained ones. Experiments on CIFAR-100, miniImageNet, and CUB-200 demonstrate average accuracy gains of +8.09%, +6.10%, and +0.04% over prior best methods.

§ 3

Core Method

01   Spatial

Spatial Mixing

Interpolates feature embeddings of odd-indexed batch samples with randomly paired peers at λ = 0.5, increasing intra-class geometric diversity within the convex hull of each class — without displacing cluster centres.

02   Spectral

Frequency Perturbation

Replaces the Fourier spectrum of spatially-mixed embeddings with that of a randomly permuted batch peer, scaled by β = 0.5. Finite-precision FFT introduces deterministic, non-invertible perturbations unavailable through spatial blending alone.

03   Stochastic

Noise Injection

Small isotropic Gaussian noise (σ = 0.02) is added before ℓ₂-normalisation, ensuring every proxy embedding is geometrically compatible with the cosine classifier and lies on the unit hypersphere 𝕊^{D−1}.

§ 6

Conclusion

We propose DA², a dataset-aware adaptive augmentation framework for FSCIL that enriches base-class representations through spatial mixing, Fourier-spectrum permutation, and blend-noise-normalisation. By measuring inter-class cosine congestion in a single forward pass, DA² automatically selects between a full multi-domain pipeline for coarse-grained datasets and spatial-only augmentation for fine-grained datasets — requiring no manual configuration, no additional parameters, and no exemplar replay.


The core insight is geometric: any fixed augmentation policy is simultaneously suboptimal for at least one dataset type. Experiments on miniImageNet, CIFAR-100, and CUB-200 confirm the hypothesis, with margins of +8.09%, +6.10%, and +0.04% over the prior best methods.
